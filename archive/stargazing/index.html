Stargazing with the rats of Sloterdijk

06.08.2024г. Iris Nikolova

The signs of God now mark the sky
And their beauty doth caress my eye

Yet, though I hear his ringing voice,
Mine eyes forsake his sacred lore;
Are wetted, glossy, glassy, absent joy’s
delightful light,
or life’s long ’n loud encore.
These thoughts, they haunt me,
taunt me, daunt me, turn my day into a chore.
They key and lock me into stasis,
turn my ill-begotten, rotten misery a lonesome bore!
Each and every painful pace is paid for by a hefty fee
for which the payment, Cherubs tell me, I could never bear afford.
But if I sack’d these sorry feelings, shed the shattered core of y’ore,
if I declared my soul insolvent, purged it of the debts of y’ore,
won’t that ghastly maiden, Justice, cleanse me of my craven,
illness-laden, my forsaken heart and core?
And won’t the fair and giving Godhead, which we in the stars observe,
impart upon me what is fairly clearly needed, nay—deserved?
Resolutely, I am answered by a chanting from above:
“NAY!” shout a thousand glowing faces,
glowing faces up above,
and they leave me kneeling, sobbing, scraping traces off the floor—
scraping traces of their graces, lacking Destiny’s rapport.
